Context

[Draft — full copy coming] A team of five designers building a cooking app around three goals: bringing people together around food, saving money, and reducing grocery waste — while helping people genuinely learn to cook.

Where we began

People want to cook more, but food and money keep slipping away.

Groceries go bad, budgets blow up, and recipes feel intimidating. We started by mapping the real friction between “I should cook” and actually getting a meal on the table.

research · the home-cooking journey

What we found

Cooking sticks when it’s social and low-stakes.

People cooked more when it felt shared and forgiving rather than precise and lonely. Saving money and cutting waste turned out to be powerful motivators — if we made the payoff visible.

“Add full quote from research here.”

Solution

Guidance, savings, and a community in one app.

The app pairs step-by-step recipe guidance with a pantry that tracks what you have, nudges you before food expires, and shows the money you’re saving — all wrapped in a social feed that makes cooking feel shared.
